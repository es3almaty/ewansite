FARABI

A risk-based framework for higher education to rebuild the credibility of assessment under AI conditions.
The problem

Students can now produce coursework with AI faster than staff can mark it. Detection tools do not work. Bans do not stick. Universities are running out of credible answers, and the pressure on graduate quality, accreditation, and institutional reputation is rising.

The real problem is not cheating. The real problem is that traditional coursework has stopped being good evidence of what students actually know.

What we do

We triage your assessment portfolio by vulnerability and impact. Some assessments are at high risk because AI can produce credible work without the student demonstrating the targeted learning. Others are low risk because the evidence remains sound. We work at the program level rather than assessment by assessment, so the changes are sustainable.

The aim is not to police AI. The aim is to rebuild assessment so it produces credible evidence under current conditions.

What you get
  • A risk-mapped view of your assessment portfolio
  • Redesign priorities and concrete recommendations
  • Governance and policy guidance
  • Staff capability support to make the changes work in real classrooms
Who this is for

Universities and higher education institutions. Accreditation and quality assurance bodies. Ministries of education running national assessment policy. Private and corporate training providers facing the same challenge.

Already built

The FARABI framework is published as an open-access working paper. A free online audit tool will analyze a single module syllabus and return a risk triage in minutes.
