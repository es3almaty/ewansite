The problem

What this fixes

Students can now produce plausible coursework with AI faster than staff can mark it. Detection tools do not solve the problem, and bans do not hold.

The real problem is not cheating in the narrow sense. The problem is that traditional coursework can stop being credible evidence of what students know.

What we do

How the engagement works

We triage assessment portfolios by vulnerability and impact.

The work identifies which assessments remain credible, which need controls, and which should be redesigned because the evidence is no longer strong enough.

The aim is to rebuild proof, not to stage a losing war against tools students already have.

What you get

Outputs

  • A risk-mapped view of the assessment portfolio
  • Redesign priorities and concrete recommendations
  • Governance and policy guidance
  • Staff capability support for implementation

Who this is for

Client fit

Universities, quality assurance bodies, ministries of education, private training providers, and corporate learning teams facing AI-era proof problems.
